## 🎯 Featured Research — VAP Detection via Acoustic Monitoring

<p align="center">
  <strong>🩺 Self-Supervised Acoustic Monitoring for Early Detection of Ventilator-Associated Pneumonia (VAP)</strong><br/>
  <em>in Mechanically Ventilated ICU Patients</em>
</p>

<table align="center" width="100%">
<tr>
<td align="left" width="50%">

**🔍 What's the problem?**
- VAP is a major ICU complication (~10-25% of ventilated patients)
- Current detection relies on clinical signs + imaging (slow, invasive)
- Need: non-invasive, real-time monitoring
- **Solution**: Acoustic biomarkers in ventilator sounds

</td>
<td align="left" width="50%">

**💡 How we solve it**
- **Self-supervised learning** on unlabeled ventilator audio
- **Contrastive methods** (SimCLR, BYOL) + Transformers
- **Spectral features** + **temporal patterns** extraction
- **Clinical validation** on real ICU data
- **Practical deployment**: Low-latency, edge-compatible

</td>
</tr>
</table>

### Key Contributions
- Novel audio-based VAP detection without labeled clinical data
- Self-supervised pre-training on continuous ventilator streams
- Temporal modeling of respiratory acoustics
- Lightweight model architecture for bedside monitoring

## 🎓 Research Philosophy

> *"The future of healthcare is not in more invasive monitoring — it's in learning from the signals that are already there."*

My research focuses on turning ambient clinical data (like ventilator sounds) into actionable intelligence using modern deep learning. By combining **self-supervised learning** with **acoustic engineering**, we can detect diseases earlier, non-invasively, and at scale.

**Goal**: Make advanced healthcare AI accessible to every ICU, in every country. 🌍
